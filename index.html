Travel Right

Simple things you can actually do when you visit a place — so you don't leave it worse than you found it.

Bring your own bag and water bottle

A reusable bag and water bottle are two of the easiest things you can pack — and they make a bigger difference than you'd think. So much of the plastic waste left behind at beaches and trails comes from single-use items tourists bring in. You don't have to be a zero-waste expert. Just swap out the disposables where you can.

If you brought it, take it back

A lot of the most beautiful spots in the Philippines are far from the nearest town — which means there's no trash collection out there. If you packed snacks, wrappers, or bottles, just carry them back out with you. It's a small inconvenience for you, and a huge one avoided for the place you just enjoyed.

Skip the plastic straw

When you're ordering food or drinks near the water or at outdoor stalls, a simple "no straw, please" goes a long way. Most vendors won't mind at all. It takes two seconds to say and costs you nothing — but those straws end up in the ocean more often than you'd expect.

Don't just scroll past it — report it

If you come across a spot that's clearly been trashed, take two minutes to file a report on TrashTalk. It's not just for the record — the reports we receive are what actually push local government units to schedule cleanups. Your post might be exactly what gets someone out there to fix it.

Support places that actually care

When you can, choose restaurants, resorts, and tour operators that make their environmental efforts visible — not just as a marketing line, but in how they actually operate. And if you leave a review, mention it. Other travelers look for that, and it helps responsible businesses stand out.

About TrashTalk

An ICT project for social change.

TrashTalk is a community-driven web platform that maps and tracks tourist trash hotspots across the Philippines. Our goal is to empower ordinary citizens to document waste problems and push for accountability from local government units (LGUs) and tourism operators.

Every report submitted on this site is logged publicly and shared with relevant local authorities. We believe that visibility is the first step toward cleanup — and that travelers have a responsibility to the places they visit.

Why This Matters

The Philippines ranks among the top contributors to ocean plastic waste globally. Much of it originates from tourist destinations — beaches, mountains, and heritage sites that millions visit each year. TrashTalk exists to make the problem visible, and to help communities hold visitors and authorities accountable.
